Unique Hotel

Join us as we embark on a journey to discover the most extraordinary, quirky, and downright magical places to lay your head in New Zealand

From treehouses nestled high in the forest canopy to underwater hotels where the ocean is your neighbor, we'll explore a myriad of accommodations that redefine the traditional hotel experience. Each post will be a window into the whimsical, the historic, and the offbeat, providing inspiration for your next adventure and a virtual escape from the ordinary.

Hapuku Lodge & Tree Houses

Who wants to remain in a treehouse, raise your hand? In stunning Kaikoura, at Hapuku Lodge & Tree Houses, let your childhood fantasies come true. The treehouses, which are ten meters above the ground and encircled by natural vegetation, have a jacuzzi, mini-bar (sundowner G&Ts, anyone? ), and a fireplace. This is a real bit of paradise, no kidding.

A Boot

This is very really cool. There's only enough space for the two of you, so you can spend the evening cuddling up in a boot. Yes, the type of shoes. The Boot Fairytale Accommodation is a completely furnished standalone cottage with a Juliet balcony, spiral staircase, and open fire that is located in the Tasman area. Have a wonderful nap, bid the day goodbye with a platter and wine in your own courtyard, and wake up to breakfast served right to your door. Yes, it is a fairytale.

A Glamping Oasis

While we acknowledge that a traditional New Zealand camping vacation is always a good idea, we also believe that staying in Queenstown's first and only geodesic dome, Seffers Dome near Ben Lomond, is far more convenient than carrying around your own tent.

This hidden gem is worth discovering since it is fully equipped with everything you could possibly need, including a gas stove, solar electricity, and a flushing toilet in addition to a hot shower. We also adore that you can completely disconnect from the outer world because there is no WiFi or coverage.
