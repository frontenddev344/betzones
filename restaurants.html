RESTAURANTS & BARS

In Sportsbookpromotions.org, we invite you to the finest culinary and cocktail experiences across the continent. This review will embark you on a tantalizing journey through Europe's vibrant gastronomic scene, where Michelin-starred restaurants, trendy cocktail bars, and hidden gems await your discovery.

From the romantic streets of Paris to the picturesque coastal towns of Italy, Europe is a treasure trove of culinary delights. Our reviews will guide you to the most acclaimed restaurants helmed by world-renowned chefs, where innovative cuisine and exquisite flavors take center stage. Whether you're craving traditional French fare, authentic Italian pasta, or modern Scandinavian cuisine, prepare to indulge your palate in a symphony of tastes and textures.

Also here at sportboookpromotions you will find Europe's best bars and lounges reviews, from historic speakeasies tucked away in hidden alleyways to rooftop terraces with panoramic views of the city skyline, our reviews will lead you to the hottest spots for expertly crafted cocktails and lively atmospheres. Whether you prefer classic concoctions or inventive mixology creations, Europe's cocktail culture is sure to delight and inspire.

Denmark

Jah Izakaya and Sake Bar, Copenhagen

These days, Copenhagen is overrun by Japanese and Korean eateries that combine the cuisine of East Asia with New Nordic philosophy. 2018 saw the opening of Jah Izakaya, a casual, lively, and welcoming gastropub with Japanese influences on a street close to the meatpacking area. It has amazing beef cars, gyoza, tonkatsu, and Danish squid.

Pomle Nakke, Horbelev

Suggested to me by a friend from Denmark, this light-filled location perched on a cliff overlooking the Baltic was once a traditional family-run eatery, but the proprietors of the Sticks'n'Sushi business transformed it into a more sophisticated dining experience. It's in an unexplored but rapidly developing area around 90 minutes south of Copenhagen. A picnic of Danish oysters, locally caught scallops, fish, and kimchi under the beech trees is a great way to round off a morning spent on the surrounding beaches littered with fossils.

Italy

La Lanterna di Diogene, near Modena, Emilia Romagna

This restaurant and working farm in the town of Solara, named for a Cynic philosopher, takes the concept of locavore extremely seriously. Pork ribs originate from the historic breed of pigs they cultivate, the pickled vegetables are grown feet from the table, and the flour for the tortellini in brodo is milled nearby. Here, following centuries-old methods, even the balsamic vinegar poured over frittatas and ricotta flans is prepared. When visiting neighboring Modena, I usually make a stop for La Lanterna and make sure I'm not the designated driver so I can savor the exhaustive variety of dry lambruscos, which are also created in the area.

France

Au Vieux de la Vieille

Lille The term "estaminet" refers to a specific type of restaurant found in northern France that originated from the days when coal workers would get together for beer and small talk after work. The estaminets are still there, and Au Vieux de la Vieille is among the best in Lille, even if the mines are long gone. It serves traditional delicacies including potjevleesch (four meats in aspic jelly, which tastes better than it sounds), beef carbonnade (made with beer and gingerbread), and dishes prepared with maroilles, the pungent local cheese. With its racks of trinkets, rustic wooden tables and chairs, and dried hops, the atmosphere adds just as much character to the experience as the cuisine. Reservations are required.

Portugal

Prado, Lisbon

Get in there first because I think this airy, light-filled restaurant in Lisbon's Chiado area will soon receive a Michelin star. Chef António Galapito, a brilliant but extremely reserved individual who worked with Nuno Mendes in London, has returned to Portugal and his food has reached new heights. While all the product is prepared with a profound competence and punky sense, it is everything as local as possible. Every time I visit Lisbon, I return and devour everything on the menu.

Belgium

Retsins Lucifernum, Brugge

Rectsins Lucifernum has been named the greatest bar in Europe for 2023! An idea that is completely macabre, absurd, and unorthodox; a place to enjoy a drink and share a life experience; a labor of passion. At the modest-looking, ominous entrance, you will find a house brimming with gothic art, antiques, and memorabilia. Dracula's Butler, with his blond hair, beckons you inside. A garden terrace and underground bar are accessible from the mansion. That's only the very beginning. This Belgian oddity can never be deemed showy because it is too unique and labor-intensive. Crazy games, great beverages, and mingling with other guests in a cozy, tiny space with no more than 20 people in attendance at once.

Hungary

Szimpla Kert, Budapest

Szimpla Kert has written a great deal about it previously; in fact, everyone who even somewhat follows the European bar scene is likely familiar with the idea of a "ruin bar," if not with the knowledge that Szimpla Kert's ingenuity and pioneering were responsible for the development of the scene

The mood and decor of Szimpla are so different from anything you've probably ever experienced that your first visit seems almost like you're walking through a doorway into a different realm. Although there are some parallels and the space is big enough to be considered a club, it seems much more laid back than that. Yes, they open in the afternoon, when the atmosphere is languid and bohemian, with people lounging around in kind of a squatter's ruin, albeit one that has been adorned.

Bosnia & Herzegovina

Zlatna Ribica, Sarajevo

With its abundance of antiques and eye-catching details, Zlatna Ribica (Gold Fish) is a site of exotic quirkiness. You may spend hours admiring its characteristics. Its sheer volume fills the whole space within the pub and even spills outside, where one of their quirky furniture pieces and devices is located on the pavement. Indeed, you will discover some golden fish, bowls atop heaps of books, elaborate vintage lampshades, and rails and sideboards coated in wax that are adorned with intricate details and crenulations. Though just decorative, the bar is really bizarre, with things placed in unexpected places and the strangest stuff popping up. The portable black-and-white TV that is perched on a toilet roll in the restrooms shouldn't worry you.

Germany

Papa Joe’s Jazz Lokal, Cologne

One of the best jazz bars in the world—warm, lively, and intelligent. Roman ruins behind glass at the entryway, animated heads on the wall, and a stage similar to a theater across from the bar. A computerized reader at the front displays the entire amount, along with a live performance every night and a cocktail maker. Luxuriously decadent, whimsical, and sensual in a manner that is exclusive to Germany. It is just too at ease in its own shoes to give a damn about whether it is trendy or not. It's impossible to stay out of the activity.

Amsterdam

Café De Dokter.

Amsterdam turns becoming a star once more. Known for its illustrious clientele, arrive early to get one of the few seats available, or settle with being a part of the lively crowd. A little yet really unique tavern, among the best old cafés. Candlelight, cobwebs, an elderly master in charge with a barrel-chested record player, and jazz music. An unforgettable first impression and a must-do experience while in Europe.
